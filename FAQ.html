What does Kulavotu mean?

The name was derived from terms originating from two languages,
Kiswahili "a native language spoken in Kenya and other parts of Africa," and English.
Directly translated The first four letters
KULA
mean to eat in kiswahili. The next two letters
VO
refering to volume a marketing term meaning "a measure of the amount of trade that has taken place, ususally in a specified period"
and the last two letters
TU
mean just,
putting the words together reads

JUST EAT THE VOLUME

What are jumps?

These are customized timeframes (15m, 45m) used on the Market Analysis page.
Each number preceeding 'jump' represents a special period
used to analyse the market carefully considering certain market conditions.
Jumps
1 jump - maximum period assessment.
5 jumps - 5 year assessment.
10 jumps - 10 weeks assessment.
12 Jumps - a year assessment.
14 jumps - Fortnight(2 weeks) assessment.

How do I use the market analysis tool?

First section

Displays a simple chart nothing fancy.
The Window size is the period you want to see the assests movement
e.g
(2w): a 2 week period
or
(4h): the past four hours.
Caution! some assest may not be available yet, Be patient.


Second section

Trims the whole process to a few statements based off the security you typed.
Once you enter the name and select what jump period to analyze,
the algorithm working behind closed doors, returns its sentiments, describing the securities movement.


Third section

An efficient portfolio system
that displays how a combination of your securitites put together
move according to the overall market sentiment and each individual security.
Parameters
Capital - amount allocated to the portfolio.
Number - how many securities you want to feed the system
Name - securities you desire to flavour your portfolio.
Profit percentage - the minimum percentage of your capital that you want to achieve from all your assets.
Risk percentage - the maximum percentage of your capital that you are willing to risk on all your assests.
